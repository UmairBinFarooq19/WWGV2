Overview: Kashmir Great Lakes Trek

The Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is widely regarded as India's most beautiful high-altitude trek — and it's easy to see why. The trail winds through seven breathtaking alpine lakes nestled in glacial cirques, surrounded by snow-capped Himalayan peaks, wildflower meadows bursting with colour, and the kind of silence that reminds you why you trekked all this way.

Starting from Sonamarg in the Ganderbal district, the trail traverses some of the most remote and spectacular landscapes in the Kashmir Himalayas before concluding at Naranag, near Manasbal Lake. Over 7–8 days, trekkers cover approximately 70–75 km at altitudes ranging from 2,600m to 4,267m.

WildWays Global — Kashmir's most experienced local trekking agency — has guided hundreds of trekkers through the Great Lakes, with a 4.9★ rating from 124+ verified trek reviews.

🏆 Why the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek? This trek covers seven high-altitude alpine lakes: Krishnasar, Vishansar, Gadsar, Satsar, Gangabal, Nundkol, and the turquoise Tarsar. No other trek in India offers this concentration of alpine beauty in a single journey.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

  1. Arrival at Sonamarg (2,800m) — Drive & Acclimatisation
    Reach Sonamarg from Srinagar (87 km, ~2.5 hrs). Meet your WildWays guide, collect trek permits, check gear. Evening briefing, overnight at Sonamarg guesthouse. Trek: 0 km
  2. Sonamarg to Nichnai (3,500m)
    The trek begins through dense pine forests and meadows following the Nichnai stream. Gradual ascent through beautiful valley. Distance: 11 km · Duration: 5–6 hrs
  3. Nichnai to Vishansar Lake (3,710m) via Nichnai Pass (4,100m)
    Cross the Nichnai Pass — dramatic views of Kashmir range. Descend to twin lakes Vishansar and Krishnasar. Camp beside shimmering blue waters. Distance: 13 km · Duration: 7–8 hrs
  4. Vishansar Lake to Gadsar Lake (3,600m) via Gadsar Pass (4,267m)
    The highest point of the trek — Gadsar Pass at 4,267m. Spectacular panoramic views. Descend through wildflower meadows to the "lake of flowers." Distance: 12 km · Duration: 7–8 hrs
  5. Gadsar Lake to Satsar Lakes (3,658m)
    Relatively gentle day through high-altitude pastures. Pass seven interconnected Satsar Lakes. Encounter local Gujjar shepherd communities. Distance: 10 km · Duration: 5–6 hrs
  6. Satsar to Gangabal Lake (3,576m)
    Arrive at the largest lake on the trek — Gangabal. Sacred to Kashmiri Pandits, it hosts the annual Harmukh Gangabal yatra. Camp between Gangabal and Nundkol. Distance: 11 km · Duration: 6–7 hrs
  7. Gangabal to Naranag (2,100m) via Trunkhal
    Final descent through ancient Naranag temple ruins (8th century). Dramatic drop from high alpine zone to the green valley. Drive back to Srinagar. Distance: 13 km · Duration: 6–7 hrs

Trek Difficulty & Fitness Requirements

The Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is rated Difficult. It is not a beginner trek. Here's what you need:

  • Good cardiovascular fitness — ability to walk 10–13 km daily with a 6–8 kg backpack
  • No prior technical climbing experience required
  • Prior trekking experience (at least 1–2 moderate treks) strongly recommended
  • Ability to handle cold temperatures (0°C to 15°C during the day; -5°C to -10°C at night in camp)
  • No history of severe altitude sickness
