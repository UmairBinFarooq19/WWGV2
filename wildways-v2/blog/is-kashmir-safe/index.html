Is Kashmir Safe for Tourists in 2025? — Honest Local Guide

📅 Updated 2025 · ⏱ 7 min read · ✍️ WildWays Global Team

This is the most-asked question we receive at WildWays Global. Our answer, as a Gulmarg-based local agency that has conducted 287+ tours since 2018: Yes — Kashmir is safe for tourists. Here's the full, honest picture.

The Tourism Numbers Tell the Story

In 2024, Kashmir received over 2.5 crore (25 million) tourists — a record. In 2023, 2.11 crore visitors. These are not small numbers. Gulmarg alone sees lakhs of visitors every ski season. The tourism infrastructure in Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, and Sonamarg is sophisticated, well-managed, and genuinely welcoming.

Reality Check: The media's portrayal of Kashmir is significantly outdated. Tourist areas have been peaceful and safe for years. The majority of our guests are first-time Kashmir visitors who arrive with concerns and leave saying "I should have come years ago."

Which Areas Are Safe for Tourists?

  • Srinagar: Very safe. Dal Lake, Mughal Gardens, Lal Chowk, old city — all tourist areas are well-secured and welcoming. Millions visit annually.
  • Gulmarg: Extremely safe. A dedicated tourist destination. WildWays is based here — we would not operate here if it weren't safe.
  • Pahalgam: Very safe. Popular family destination. No tourist incidents in recent years.
  • Sonamarg: Safe. Very scenic. Military presence along the highway is visible but not intimidating.

Practical Safety Tips for Kashmir

  1. Stay in tourist areas — there is absolutely no reason to venture into restricted border areas
  2. Register with your hotel (legally required) and share your itinerary with family
  3. Travel with a reputable local agency like WildWays Global — we monitor conditions daily
  4. Download offline maps and save emergency numbers
  5. Carry a photocopy of your ID at all times (as you would anywhere in India)
  6. Be respectful of local customs, especially around mosques and during prayer times

Yes — Kashmir is safe for solo female travellers. Many women travel solo through Srinagar, Gulmarg, and Pahalgam every year. Kashmiris are famously hospitable. We recommend booking with a reputable agency like WildWays Global for solo trips to ensure you have a point of contact and reliable transport throughout.

We always recommend travel insurance for any Indian or international destination. For Kashmir, the main insurance considerations are weather-related flight delays (Srinagar airport closes in heavy fog), and trip cancellation due to road closures in winter. Basic travel insurance covering these events is recommended.
