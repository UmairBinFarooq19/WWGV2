Kashmir Great Lakes Trek — Complete 2025 Trekker's Guide

📅 Updated 2025 · ⏱ 10 min read · ✍️ WildWays Global Team

The Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is India's most spectacular high-altitude trek — a 70+ km journey through seven pristine alpine lakes, glaciated passes, wildflower meadows, and remote Himalayan landscapes that feel genuinely untouched.

This is the definitive guide to the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek in 2025 — written by WildWays Global, the Gulmarg-based trekking agency that has guided hundreds of trekkers through this route.

At a Glance: Duration: 7–8 days | Distance: 70–75 km | Max Altitude: 4,267m (Gadsar Pass) | Start: Sonamarg | End: Naranag | Best Time: July–September | Cost: ₹14,999–₹22,999

Why Do the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek?

India has many famous treks — Roopkund, Chadar, Markha Valley — but the Kashmir Great Lakes is unique in its concentration of alpine lakes. Seven distinct lakes in a single continuous trail, each with its own character:

  • Krishnasar & Vishansar: Twin lakes at 3,800m, surrounded by snow peaks and reflecting the sky like mirrors
  • Gadsar: The "Lake of Flowers" — meadows of alpine wildflowers in July–August
  • Satsar: Seven interconnected lakes with Gujjar shepherd encampments
  • Gangabal & Nundkol: The sacred lakes at the base of Mt. Harmukh (5,142m) — the trek's dramatic finale

Kashmir Great Lakes Trek Difficulty

This trek is rated Difficult. Be honest with yourself before booking:

  • Daily distances: 10–13 km with elevation changes of 500–1,200m
  • Highest pass: Gadsar Pass at 4,267m — requires good acclimatisation
  • Weather: Can change rapidly; afternoon thunderstorms common in August
  • No technical climbing, but trekking poles are strongly advised

Month-by-Month: Best Time for the Trek

  • July: Snow melted, wildflowers at peak, lakes fully visible. Best photography. Some pass snow.
  • August: Best overall conditions. Clearest visibility, warmest nights. Busiest month.
  • September: Fewer trekkers, autumn colours beginning. Lakes still accessible until mid-September.
  • After September: Snow returns to passes by late September. Trek not advisable.

How to Reach the Trailhead (Sonamarg)

Sonamarg is 87 km from Srinagar (2.5–3 hours by cab). WildWays Global includes pickup from Srinagar/Gulmarg as part of all trek packages. Alternatively, shared taxis from Srinagar's Batmaloo bus stand run to Sonamarg for ₹400–₹600 per person.

Not recommended without at least one prior moderate trek (Kedarkantha, Hampta Pass, or similar). The daily distances, altitude, and high-altitude pass crossings make this unsuitable for complete beginners. We advise 4–6 weeks of preparation including daily 5–8 km walks with a loaded backpack.

Mobile network (Jio/Airtel) is available at Sonamarg and the Naranag endpoint, but is mostly absent on the trail. BSNL sometimes has signal at higher camps. We recommend downloading offline maps (Maps.me or AllTrails) before starting the trek.

No formal tea houses exist on the Kashmir Great Lakes route. Gujjar shepherd families occasionally sell chai and basic snacks at some camps, but you cannot rely on this. All meals are provided by WildWays Global as part of the trek package.
