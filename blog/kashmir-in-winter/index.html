Kashmir in Winter — Complete Snow & Skiing Guide 2025

📅 Updated 2025 · ⏱ 6 min read · ✍️ WildWays Global Team

Winter Kashmir is magical — snowfall transforms Gulmarg into a world-class ski resort, Srinagar's Dal Lake occasionally freezes, and the silence of snow-covered Himalayan meadows is profoundly peaceful. If you've ever dreamed of a white Kashmir holiday, December to February is your season.

What to Expect: Kashmir Winter Weather

  • Gulmarg: -5°C to 5°C daytime; -15°C to -8°C at night. Snowfall 3–4 metres total. Best ski conditions January–February.
  • Srinagar: -2°C to 8°C daytime; -8°C to -4°C nights. Occasional snowfall. Roads occasionally disrupted.
  • Pahalgam: -5°C to 3°C. Very cold. Limited tourism. Some guesthouses closed.

❄️ When does Gulmarg get snow? First significant snowfall typically arrives in late November. January and February see the heaviest snowfall. March is spring skiing — snow still good but days longer and warmer.

Top Things to Do in Kashmir in Winter

  1. Gulmarg Skiing: India's best ski resort. All skill levels. WildWays provides ski lessons, gear rental, and gondola passes. See our skiing packages.
  2. Gulmarg Gondola to Apharwat (4,390m): Spectacular snow-covered views. Phase 1 and Phase 2 operational during ski season.
  3. Snowmobiling & ATV Rides: Race across Gulmarg's snow-covered meadows.
  4. Dal Lake in Winter: Srinagar's iconic lake in winter has a unique frozen beauty. Shikara rides continue even in cold weather.
  5. Snow Trekking: Short guided snow treks from Gulmarg to Khilanmarg.

What to Pack for Kashmir in Winter

  • Thermal base layers (top and bottom) — at least 2 sets
  • Heavy-duty waterproof jacket and ski pants
  • Snow boots (waterproof, insulated)
  • Warm gloves, wool socks, balaclava, snow goggles
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ (snow reflects UV strongly)
  • Lip balm and moisturiser (extreme dryness)
