Best Time to Visit Kashmir — Complete 2025 Guide

📅 Updated 2025 · ⏱ 6 min read · ✍️ WildWays Global Team

Kashmir is beautiful in every season — but the "best time" depends entirely on what you want to experience. Snow lover? Flower chaser? Trekker? Here's an honest month-by-month breakdown from our local team based in Gulmarg.

December to February — Winter Kashmir & Skiing

This is Gulmarg's skiing season and Kashmir at its most magical. Gulmarg receives 3–4 metres of snowfall, transforming the meadows into a pristine white wonderland. The Gulmarg gondola operates to Apharwat Peak where snow depths exceed 2 metres.

  • Gulmarg: Exceptional. World-class skiing, snowfall, gondola. January is peak.
  • Srinagar: Cold (0°C to -5°C). Dal Lake sometimes freezes. Magical but cold.
  • Pahalgam: Very cold. Some roads may be closed. Not ideal unless you want isolation.
  • Trekking: Not possible (all treks closed due to snow).

❄️ Best for: Skiing, snowboarding, snow photography, romantic winter escapes. Book 60+ days in advance — December and January slots fill up fast with WildWays Global.

March to April — Transition & Spring Awakening

March brings the first hints of spring — snow still covers Gulmarg (great for skiing), while Srinagar's chinar trees begin to bud. April is when the famous Tulip Garden in Srinagar (Asia's largest) bursts into colour.

  • Best for: Spring skiing, tulip garden (April), budget travel (off-peak rates)
  • Temperature: 5°C–18°C

May to June — Peak Season & Perfect Weather

This is Kashmir's most popular season. Temperatures are ideal (18°C–28°C), Gulmarg meadows are carpeted with wildflowers, and all tourist destinations are fully accessible. The downside: heavy crowds and peak pricing.

  • Gulmarg: Green meadows, flowers, gondola fully operational. Beautiful.
  • Srinagar: Best weather. Mughal Gardens at their finest.
  • Pahalgam: Lush green valleys. River rafting season begins.
  • Sonamarg: Accessible. Glacier visits by pony.

🌸 Best for: First-time Kashmir visitors, families, honeymooners. Book at least 3 months in advance for May–June.

July to September — Trekking Season

The Kashmir Great Lakes Trek season. Snow melts to reveal 7 pristine alpine lakes, wildflower meadows, and high Himalayan passes. Daytime temperatures are pleasant at higher altitudes (12°C–22°C). The valley gets some rain (light monsoon) but Gulmarg remains beautiful.

  • Best for: Kashmir Great Lakes Trek, Tarsar Marsar Trek, camping, adventure
  • Temperature: 20°C–30°C in Srinagar; 12°C–20°C at Gulmarg

October to November — Golden Autumn

Kashmir's most photogenic season. The chinar trees turn gold, saffron fields bloom purple in Pampore, and the skies are crystal clear. Crowds thin out, hotels offer 20–30% discounts, and the entire valley glows with autumn colour.

  • Best for: Photography, budget travel, peaceful holidays, autumn foliage
  • Temperature: 8°C–22°C

There is no truly "bad" time to visit Kashmir, but heavy rain periods in July–August can affect road conditions and make trekking harder. January nights in Srinagar can be extremely cold (-8°C). Plan accordingly.

Kashmir receives snowfall from late November through March. Gulmarg typically sees the first significant snowfall in late November. Peak snowfall is in January. Srinagar at lower altitude sees snow 2–4 times per season.
